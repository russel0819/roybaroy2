Marcos: Success of food stamp program means hunger-free PH

By Ruth Abbey Gita-Carlos

September 29, 2023, 5:19 pm


RICE AID. President Ferdinand R. Marcos Jr. delivers a speech during a rice distribution activity in Siargao Island, Surigao del Norte on Friday (Sept. 29, 2023). A total of 2,265 Pantawid Pamilyang Pilipino Program beneficiaries from Siargao Island and 1,000 from Dinagat Islands received the sacks of rice, part of the seizure of the Bureau of Customs during a warehouse raid in Zamboanga City early this month. (PNA photo by Joey Razon)

MANILA - President Ferdinand R. Marcos Jr. has instructed government agencies to ensure the integrity of the "Walang Gutom 2027: Food Stamp Program (FSP)." At the program's launch in Siargao Island, Marcos emphasized his commitment to eradicating hunger in the Philippines. He directed concerned agencies to collaborate for the proper implementation of the FSP and ensure transparency and accountability. The program aims to provide nutritious food to disadvantaged households, promoting not just fullness but also health and vitality. EBT cards, loaded with PHP3,000 worth of food credits, were distributed to beneficiaries, with allocations for carbohydrates, protein, and fruits/vegetables. Additionally, Marcos highlighted efforts to combat hoarding and smuggling of agricultural products to stabilize rice prices.

President Marcos also directed local government units to cease collecting toll fees and charges from cargo vehicles transporting goods, facilitating ease of doing business and lowering transportation costs. The government, together with private organizations, aims to ensure that the food stamp program achieves its goal of reducing malnutrition and poverty in the country by providing meal augmentation to food-poor families and helping them become productive citizens.
