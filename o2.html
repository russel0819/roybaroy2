Federalism and the 1973 Constitution

GLIMPSES & GAZES

By Severino C. Samonte

September 28, 2023


President Ferdinand R. Marcos Jr. has expressed support for a shift towards federalism in the Philippines, aiming to create a more stable political structure. He believes that decentralizing power to various regions in the country is essential to ensure stability. This move towards federalism echoes the concept of having multiple power centers rather than centralizing everything in Manila. According to President Marcos Jr., such decentralization is crucial because it allows decision-making to occur at lower levels of government.

This discussion on federalism has prompted some to look back at the 1973 Constitution, which introduced a modified parliamentary form of government in the Philippines. Under this system, the president was assisted by a prime minister and a Cabinet closely connected to the legislative body known as the Batasang Pambansa. This arrangement was designed to prevent legislative deadlocks and ensure smoother government operations. The 1973 Constitution was in place for 13 years before being replaced by the 1987 Constitution following the People Power Revolution in 1986, which restored the bicameral Congress with the House of Representatives and the Senate.
