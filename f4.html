Putting the spotlight on Bicolano artists

By Gladys Serafica

September 21, 2023, 9:01 pm


BICOL’S FINEST.Local art luminaries Noel Muñoz, Felipe Peralta, Francisco Mitra and Frank Penoñes Jr. (left to right) top bill Bicol University’s cultural show, "Suro: Pasale nin Kulturang Bikolnon" to celebrate the region’s rich artistry and vibrant cultural heritage. (PNA photo by Gladys Serafica)

LEGAZPI CITY – In a world where artificial intelligence is influencing art, four local artists from Bicol are highlighting the importance of human creativity in shaping their region's cultural identity. Frank Peñones Jr., known as "FPJ," uses the Bikol language in his poetry, film, and visual arts to preserve his heritage and advocate for equal representation. He believes that language is vital in combating marginalization in a region struggling with economic progress. FPJ's creative activism empowers and raises awareness, making him a literary hero in Bicol.

Similarly, Francisco "Keeks" Mitra and Noel "Nong" Muñoz, often compared to Simon & Garfunkel, use music to awaken love for their region. Inspired by a bus trip, Muñoz started writing Bikol songs to give their culture a voice in the music industry. Their music tells Bicolanos' stories, and their iconic song "Bikolano Ka, Bikolano Ako" became a symbol of resilience. In a world dominated by Western music, they prove that Bicolano artists have a meaningful place in the industry. Felipe "Ping" Peralta, a versatile artist, found his passion through a box of crayons and has since become a prominent figure in visual arts and journalism, sharing the beauty of the Bicol region through his work. These four artists, now in their senior years, continue to inspire the younger generation to celebrate their culture through art, demonstrating that art can make a difference, one creation at a time.
