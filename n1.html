Comelec files 35 DQ petitions vs BSKE bets for premature campaigning

By Stephanie Sevillano

September 29, 2023, 6:48 pm


DISQUALIFICATION PETITION. The Commission on Elections Task Force “Anti-Epal” files 35 disqualification petitions against candidates of the Barangay and Sangguniang Kabataan Elections allegedly involved in premature campaigning at the poll body’s main office in Intramuros, Manila on Friday (Sept. 29, 2023). The petitions shall undergo summary proceedings and the decisions will be out before the Oct. 30 polls. (Screengrab from Comelec Facebook livestream)

MANILA - The Commission on Elections (Comelec) Task Force "Anti-Epal" has taken action against 35 candidates running in the Barangay and Sangguniang Kabataan Elections (BSKE) by filing disqualification petitions. The decision on these petitions will be made public before the BSKE scheduled for October 30. Comelec spokesperson John Rex Laudiangco emphasized the seriousness of enforcing election laws and warned that more candidates could face charges next week. The violations being monitored include improper campaign materials such as oversized leaflets, posters, tarpaulins, and banners, as well as the distribution of campaign giveaways, which may be considered vote-buying under the election guidelines. Those found guilty of criminal or election offenses may not only be disqualified but also face imprisonment.

To ensure a fair and credible BSKE, Comelec is collaborating with various government agencies, including the Public Attorney's Office, the Department of National Defense, the Armed Forces of the Philippines, the Philippine National Police, the Philippine Coast Guard, and the Department of Education. These efforts are aimed at preventing vote buying and selling and enhancing monitoring across the nation, with a commitment to upholding the law and holding erring candidates accountable for their actions.
