BSP: Banks' reserve requirement ratio cut 'off the table' this year

By Anna Leah Gonzales

September 29, 2023, 9:23 am


Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP) Governor Eli Remolona Jr.

MANILA - Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP) Governor Eli Remolona Jr. has indicated that a further reduction in banks' reserve requirement ratio (RRR) is not on the table for the remainder of this year. Remolona stated that the earliest possibility for a cut in RRR could be in 2024, emphasizing that the current rate of 9.5 percent is relatively high compared to regional standards. He explained that the high reserve requirement was a traditional method to control money supply, but it now acts as a distortion in financial intermediation, creating unnecessary gaps between lending and deposit rates.

While ruling out a further RRR cut for this year, Remolona mentioned a slight possibility of an off-cycle rate hike, depending on upcoming economic indicators. He noted that the BSP's Monetary Board has maintained policy rates for four consecutive meetings, holding the target reverse repurchase (RRP) rate at 6.25 percent. The BSP had previously increased policy rates by a total of 425 basis points to address inflation, which stood at 5.3 percent in August. The governor emphasized the need to find the right balance in monetary policies to ensure stability and economic growth. The official September 2023 inflation data will be released by the Philippine Statistics Authority next week.
