PNP: Revised media policy doesn't curtail access to information

By Christopher Lloyd Caliwan

September 29, 2023, 6:22 pm


PNP spokesperson Col. Jean Fajardo (PNA file photo by Lloyd Caliwan)

MANILA - The Philippine National Police (PNP) clarified that their revised guidelines on sharing information from police reports do not limit the media's right to information. PNP spokesperson Col. Jean Fajardo explained that the purpose is to ensure that the right information is provided and that disclosing it won't hinder ongoing investigations. This clarification came after concerns were raised by the National Union of Journalists of the Philippines (NUJP) about the Negros Occidental Police Provincial Office withholding suspects' and victims' names in reports, in accordance with the PNP's Revised Media Relations Policy. Fajardo emphasized that the guidelines aim to establish a consistent approach to releasing information and comply with the Data Privacy Act, while also promoting better relations between the police and the media.

Fajardo noted that there are situations, like cases of rape and violence, where names cannot be disclosed until charges are filed in court. However, once cases are filed, this information becomes part of the public record. The PNP is taking extra precautions to protect individuals' privacy rights and prevent unauthorized disclosure of names, as they have faced legal challenges in the past related to this issue.
