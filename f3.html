Banaan museum relives rich history, culture of Pangasinan

By Hilda Austria

September 24, 2023, 6:21 pm


HISTORICAL.The provincial government inaugurates the Banaan Pangasinan Provincial Museum at Casa Real in Lingayen on Sept. 8, 2023. The museum entrance is free for the rest of September. (Photo courtesy of Pangasinan Provincial Tourism and Cultural Affairs Office)

LINGAYEN, Pangasinan â€“ The Banaan Pangasinan Provincial Museum is now open to the public, offering residents and visitors a chance to explore the rich history and culture of the province. This modern and interactive museum, located at the historic Casa Real, showcases 11 galleries with knowledgeable tour guides. Visitors can learn about various aspects of Pangasinan's heritage, including the time of Princess Urduja, galleon trade, American colonialism, World War II, and the significance of salt in the province's history. The museum also celebrates local artists and artisans, making it a must-visit destination for both students and tourists. Entrance is free throughout September in honor of National Tourism Month and Philippine Creative Industries Month.

The Banaan museum is a collaborative effort involving the provincial government, cultural institutions, and historians. It highlights both the glory and dark moments of the province's history, with exhibits ranging from old boats and trains to artifacts from World War II. Pangasinan Governor Ramon Guico III expressed his pride in the museum, emphasizing the importance of preserving the identity, culture, and history of Pangasinan. To ensure an intimate and informative experience, visitor numbers are limited per tour group, and online reservations can be made. Don't miss this opportunity to discover the fascinating heritage of Pangasinan at the Banaan Pangasinan Provincial Museum.
